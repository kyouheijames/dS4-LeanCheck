## Honest bottom line
- The theory is CONSISTENT with ALL current observations — i.e. NOT ruled out. (Weak: low bar.)
- ONE genuine qualitative success: near-scale-invariance (n_s≈1) is NATURAL here (principal-series
  base), not tuned. The graviton being effectively massless is also natural (Hubble-tied).
- Its DISTINCTIVE, falsifiable content (the cosmological-collider oscillation at a specific frequency,
  and the scalar–tensor correlation) is UNTESTED — it awaits future data (CMB-S4, LiteBIRD, LSS).
- NO current observation CONFIRMS the theory beyond consistency, and the magnitudes that would make
  it a precision prediction (n_s value, graviton mass, collider frequency/amplitude) are OPEN — gated
  on the 1/N coefficient A we mapped but could not validate.

## What would actually test it (the honest discriminators)
1. A cosmological-collider signal (oscillating bispectrum) at a frequency set by a single μ — and its
   ABSENCE at high precision would constrain/disfavour the heavy-field content.
2. A scalar↔tensor correlation tracing one ε — independent measurement of both would test the unifying
   claim. (Currently untestable.)
3. Pinning A ⇒ turning n_s, graviton mass, collider amplitude from "consistent" into NUMBERS that
   either match or fail. That is the gate between "viable framework" and "tested theory."

Status: a viable, not-ruled-out framework with a clear future test — NOT a currently-confirmed theory.
